# How to Design a Laundry

**Source:** [Bunnings New Zealand](https://www.bunnings.co.nz/diy-advice/home-improvement/design-inspiration/how-to-design-a-laundry)

---

## Overview

Planning a laundry involves considering plumbing locations, storage needs, appliance dimensions, and power requirements. A well-designed laundry can significantly improve functionality and daily life.

---

## Key Planning Considerations

### 1. Plumbing Location

- The key to your layout is where the plumbing is currently located
- Taps and waste outlets can be moved, but keeping them in place saves significant money on plumbing bills
- Plan your layout around existing plumbing infrastructure

### 2. Measurements & Dimensions

- Measure all key laundry appliances before planning
- Measure storage units and furniture
- Create a comprehensive list of items to store:
    - Brooms
    - Ironing board
    - Vacuum cleaner
    - Other cleaning supplies

### 3. Storage Planning

- **Never have too much storage** - prioritize adequate storage space
- Plan for both immediate needs and future requirements
- Consider vertical storage solutions
- Include storage for all cleaning equipment and supplies

### 4. Power Points

- Install one power point for each appliance
- Include power point for iron if planning to iron in the laundry
- Position power points strategically for easy access and safety
- Proper power point placement can dramatically improve laundry functionality

---

## Layout Considerations

### Appliance Placement

- Position washing machine near plumbing
- Consider whether front-loading or top-loading machine affects space differently
- Allow adequate space for appliance doors to open fully
- Plan for dryer placement if needed

### Work Space

- Include bench space for sorting and folding
- Ensure adequate clearance around appliances
- Plan for workflow efficiency

---

## Additional Features to Consider

- Laundry tub/sink
- Hanging space for drying
- Ironing facilities
- Natural lighting
- Ventilation
- Splashback protection
- Flooring suitable for wet areas

---

## Budget Tips

- Keeping plumbing in existing locations saves money
- Plan power points in initial design to avoid costly retrofitting
- Maximize existing infrastructure where possible
